    Suge Knight is an American former music executive and a rap industry businessman. Suge Knight is the co-founder and CEO of ‘Death Row Records.’ Moreover, he has worked with Dr. Dre, Snoop Dogg, and Tupac Shakur.

    Early Life

    His name is Marion Hugh “Suge” Knight Jr. Suge was born on April 19, 1965, in Compton, California, USA. Currently, he is 58 years old and his birth sign is Aries. He is the youngest of three siblings. He has sisters named Karen Anderson and Charlinda Tubbs.

    His parents are Maxine (mother) and Marion Knight Sr. (father). He attended ‘Lynwood High School’ in nearby Lynwood. There, he was a football and track star. Moreover, he graduated in the year 1983.

    Career and Assault

    Earlier, Suge was a football lineman and bodyguard who went on to found ‘Death Row Records’ with rapper/producer Dr. Dre. Along with the label involved in several controversies, he eventually was sentenced to prison for violating parole on the evening in which ‘Tupac Shakur’ was killed. Later, he faced financial issues and more jail time.

    Moreover, he was a central figure in gangsta rap’s catapult to massive commercialization. Therefore, this feat is attributed to the record label’s first two album releases Dr. Dre’s ‘The Chronic’ and Snoop Dogg’s ‘Doggystyle’ in 1992 and 1993 respectively. He played college football at the ‘University of Nevada, Las Vegas (UNLV)’ before beginning his music career.

    Then, he played defensive end for the Los Angeles Rams in the year 1987 during the player strike as a replacement player. In 1995, Tupac Shakur started a prison sentence of up to four and a half years. He struck a deal with him that October, paying his bail and freeing him from prison, pending his conviction’s appeal,while signing him to ‘Death Row Records.’ And, in 1996, the label released 2Pac’s greatest commercial success ‘All Eyez on Me.’

    More…

    In September, after departing a Mike Tyson boxing match in Las Vegas, a group that included him and Shakur assaulted Orlando Anderson, a Southside Compton Crips gang member. And, after 3 hours, someone shot into the car that he was driving and fatally wounded Shakur, and Anderson has since become the prime suspect.

    Further, in the aftermath of Shakur’s death, Dr. Dre and Snoop Dogg left ‘Death Row Records.’ Eventually, the label declined, and it was soon eclipsed. Similarly, allegations mounted that he, beyond employing gang members, had frequently plied intimidation and violence in his business dealings.

    And, from the late 1990s into the early 2000s, he spent a few years incarcerated for assault convictions and associated violations of probation and parole. Moreover, in September 2018, on pleading no contest to voluntary manslaughter in a fatal 2015 hit-and-run, he was sentenced to 28 years in prison.

    He is scheduled to become eligible for parole in July 2037. Moreover, following his 2015 arrest, he has experienced many health problems, including being hospitalized for blindness. Also, in 2017, blood clots. Therefore, his health issues have prevented him from attending his courting hearings.
