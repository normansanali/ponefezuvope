    Former Man City and Juventus striker Valeri Bojinov returns to Bulgaria

    Much-travelled Bulgarian forward Valeri Bojinov has returned home to join top-flight newcomers Botev Vratsa until the end of the year after leaving Croatian club HNK Rijeka last month in a bid to resurrect his flagging career.

    13 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Much-travelled Bulgarian forward Valeri Bojinov has returned home to join top-flight newcomers Botev Vratsa until the end of the year after leaving Croatian club HNK Rijeka last month in a bid to resurrect his flagging career.

    The 32-year-old was widely considered one of the most promising players in Italy when he moved to Fiorentina from Lecce for a fee of £11.6m in January, 2005.

    He also played for Juventus on loan.

    Valeri Bojinov has returned to Bulgaria to join top-flight newcomers Botev Vratsa

    The Bulgarian became the youngest non-Italian to play in Serie A when he made his debut as a 15-year-old on January 27, 2002.


    '(I feel) in Vratsa like I'm in New York,' the former Manchester City and Sporting striker said after signing a contract with Vratsa-based Botev.
