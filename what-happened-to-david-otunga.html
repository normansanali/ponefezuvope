    David Daniel Otunga, an American actor, lawyer, and former professional wrestler, has had a diverse career in the entertainment industry.

    From his early days in WWE to his transition into legal advisory roles and non-wrestling positions, Otunga's journey has been marked by various milestones and changes.

    Together with Fan Arch, we'll delve into the details of what happened to David Otunga, exploring his professional wrestling career, his foray into legal advisory and commentary roles, and his non-wrestling endeavors.

    Professional Wrestling Career

    David Otunga's professional wrestling career was marked by significant achievements and memorable moments. He signed a developmental contract with WWE in 2008 and worked in FCW before moving to NXT.

    Notably, Otunga was the runner-up on the first season of NXT. He then became an original member of The Nexus, a faction that left a lasting impact on the WWE universe.

    As part of The Nexus, Otunga became a two-time WWE Tag Team Champion, solidifying his presence in the wrestling world.

    The Nexus (2010-2011)

    During his time with The Nexus, Otunga was part of several high-profile storylines and matches, including a notable feud with John Cena.

    The faction's clash with established superstars and their involvement in championship pursuits added depth to Otunga's wrestling career.

    After The Nexus, Otunga's trajectory in wrestling took various turns, leading to his eventual retirement from in-ring competition in 2015.

    Legal Advisory and Commentary Roles

    Following his retirement from wrestling, Otunga ventured into legal advisory and commentary roles within WWE.

    He became an on-screen character, offering his legal services to fellow wrestlers and participating in storylines involving general managers and on-screen personalities.

    He appeared on RAW, SmackDown, and Main Event as a commentator and panelist, contributing to the overall presentation of WWE programming.
