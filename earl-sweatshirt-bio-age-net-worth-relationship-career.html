    Earl Sweatshirt is the stage name of American rapper and record producer Thebe Neruda Kgositsile. He is a member of the hip-hop group Odd Future, along with Tyler the Creator.

    His other identities include Sly Tendencies, Randomblackdude, DJ Earl Fletcher, and Dar Qness. He is usually dubbed a “hip-hop prodigy.” Earl earned notoriety and critical praise with the success of his debut mixtape, Earl, published in March 2010.

    At the time, he was just 16 years old. He was named “the most intriguing upcoming rapper” in 2011. He just released his third studio album, Some Rap Songs, inspired by his father’s passing.

    What is the Net Worth of Earl Sweatshirt? Salary, Earnings

    Earl Sweatshirt is an American rapper and composer. Earl Sweatshirt has a net worth of $5 million. In 2010, he released his first mixtape “Earl,” which was followed by the albums “Doris,” “I Don’t Like Shit, I Don’t Go Outside,” and “Some Rap Songs.”

    Sweatshirt was also a member of Odd Future, a hip-hop collective created in 2007 by Tyler, the Creator and other rappers.

    Where was Earl Sweatshirt born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

    Earl Sweatshirt’s true name is Thebe Neruda Kgositsile, born in South Africa. He was born on February 24, 1994, in Chicago, Illinois, in the United States.

    He was born to Keorapetse Kgositsile and Cheryl Harris. Earl’s mother was a law professor at the University of California, Los Angeles, and his father was a poet and political activist from South Africa.

    Earl’s younger brother’s name is Randy Mafalanka. His father died of illness on January 3, 2018, at the age of 79, in South Africa. He is of African-American and black ancestry. His zodiac sign is Pisces.

    He attended UCLA Lab School in Los Angeles. Earl then attended New Roads High School and Middle School in Santa Monica. Earl started rapping when he was quite young.

    How did Earl Sweatshirt start his Professional Career?

    • Earl Sweatshirt’s career began with creating and distributing songs on his MySpace account. Back then, he went by the stage name Sly Tendencies and formed The Backpackerz with his mates Loofy and Jw Mijo. The band split up soon after, in 2009.
    • Earl was discovered by Tyler the Creator in 2009 and joined the hip-hop collective ‘Odd Future.’ He changed his name to Earl Sweatshirt after joining the group.
    • Earl released his first official mixtape, Earl, when he was 16 years old, gaining him notoriety and critical praise.
    • Despite the positive response, his mother forbade him from continuing to produce songs with Odd Future.
    • To assist him in overcoming his bad habits, his mother enrolled him in Coral Reef Academy in Samoa, a therapeutic retreat school.
    • He returned to the United States in 2012 and released a preview of his new track. His return was announced on his Twitter account.
    • Earl appeared on “Oldie” from Odd Future’s debut album, The OF Tape Vol. 2.
    • Earl launched his record label, Tan Cressida, in April 2012.
    • After returning from Samoa, Earl released his first solo song, “Chum,” in November 2012.
    • Doris, originally known as Gnossos, was released as his debut album in August 2013. The album received excellent reviews and debuted at number five on the US Billboard 200. It also debuted at number one in the United States on the Billboard Top Rap Albums list.
    • Earl released his second studio album, “I Don’t Like Shit, I Don’t Go Outside,” in March 2015.
    • Earl rose to prominence after uploading a ten-minute tune named “Solace” to YouTube in April 2015.
